## Competitive Landscape

### Samsung
A global electronics giant, Samsung is Apple's primary competitor in smartphones, wearables, and tablets, offering a wide range of devices powered by Android and its own Tizen OS. They compete fiercely on innovation, premium features, and market share across multiple product categories.

### Alphabet (Google)
As the parent company of Google, Alphabet competes with Apple through its Android mobile operating system, its Pixel line of smartphones, and the Google Play Store, directly rivaling Apple's iOS, iPhone, and App Store ecosystems. They also compete in AI, cloud services, and other software offerings.

### Huawei
A Chinese multinational technology company, Huawei competes with Apple primarily in the smartphone market, especially in China, with its own HarmonyOS and a range of devices. Despite international sanctions, it maintains a strong domestic presence and continues to innovate in telecommunications and consumer electronics.

### Xiaomi
A rapidly growing Chinese electronics company, Xiaomi competes with Apple by offering a broad portfolio of smartphones, smart home devices, and wearables, particularly dominating the mid-range smartphone segment. It challenges Apple by providing feature-rich products at competitive price points, especially in Asian and emerging markets.

### Amazon
While known for e-commerce, Amazon competes with Apple in hardware through its Kindle tablets, which rival the iPad, and in the broader tech ecosystem with its Alexa AI and cloud services (AWS). They also compete for consumer attention and platform dominance.
